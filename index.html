About

Jackielou Pareja, a graduate of Bachelor of Fine Arts in Industrial Design at the University of the Philippines, Cebu, is currently pursuing Multimedia Program at Lethbridge College in Alberta, Canada. Her choice to join the College was fueled by a quest to develop skills in animation and coding, complementing her existing expertise. Jackie's inclination towards art and design emerged early, garnering her the "Best in Arts" award in elementary school and recognition as the "Artist of the Year" upon high school graduation. Her artistic journey saw further triumph when she clinched AirAsia’s esteemed "Best Choice" award in the Manila Bulletin Sketchfest, centered around the theme "Saving Earth," with over 400 participants from Cebu.

Hometown

The second-largest metropolitan area in the Philippines, Cebu is known as a fishing village before the Spanish invasion. The name “Cebu” has been interpreted in many ways, its rich history indicates that it is derived from the term “Sibu,” which means “Trade.” Because of its coastal location, was referred to as "Sinibuayng Hingpit," which translates to "the place for trading." Later, the locals became accustomed to abbreviating and using Sibu or Sibo (to deal). As a result of its gradual industrialization throughout time, Cebu province is currently among the most developed in the Philippines.
